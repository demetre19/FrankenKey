Repeatedly choosing “I'm” for “Im” now teaches FrankenKey the apostrophe preference. After four accepted corrections, pressing Space applies the learned contraction automatically while preserving the capitalized surface.

Learned words remain protected from ordinary spelling replacements such as “omp” → “mop”; alternatives remain visible and tappable. An unlearned “im” can still use the bundled contraction immediately, while learned “im” without apostrophe evidence remains literal.

Short words can be taught by immediately undoing an unwanted correction, and Backspace deletes exactly one trailing space even when an editor reports deletion incorrectly.

Common missing-apostrophe contractions such as “theyll” → “they’ll” and “well” → “we’ll” remain available for suggestions and personalization.
